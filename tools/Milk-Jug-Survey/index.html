Milk Jug Photogrammetry Traverse Tool

Estimate distances and plot a traverse using photos of a known-size object — for when you've lost your survey equipment but still have a phone, a compass, and a milk jug.

How it works
  1. Calibrate once. Set a jug at exactly 10 ft (or any distance you can measure with a tape), photograph it, then drag across the widest part of the jug in the photo. The app derives your phone's effective focal length from that. Do this in similar light conditions to your actual survey.
  2. Photo each segment. Walk to each jug station, photograph the next jug forward, and drag across it. Optionally photograph back at the previous jug — if both distances agree within ~3%, you have a solid measurement. If they disagree by more than 8%, retake in better light or retrace your steps.
  3. Enter headings. If you read a compass bearing at each station, the map draws the actual traverse with real geometry. Without headings, it just chains distances in a straight line for reference.
Field tips: Photograph jugs straight-on, not at an angle — oblique shots make the jug appear narrower and overestimate distance. Shade the jug from direct sun with your body if it's blowing out the highlights; a shadowed jug against a dark background is ideal. The back-check is especially valuable at a river crossing — photograph from each bank back toward the other, and average the two readings. On windy days, hang the jug from a branch with a short tether and take several shots quickly, then use the sharpest one.
